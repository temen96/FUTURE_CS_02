PHISHING EMAIL DETECTION & AWARENESS REPORT

1. INTRODUCTION

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where attackers try to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or personal data. These attacks are usually carried out through fake emails that appear to come from trusted sources. The goal is to manipulate users into clicking malicious links or providing confidential information.

2. EMAIL SAMPLES

Email 1:
Subject: Urgent: Your Account Will Be Locked  
Link: http://secure-account-verify.com  

Email 2:
Subject: Congratulations! You Won a Prize  
Link: http://claim-your-reward-now.com  

Email 3:
Subject: Security Alert: Suspicious Login Attempt  
Link: http://account-security-check.com  

3. ANALYSIS

a) Link Analysis  
All three emails contain suspicious links that do not belong to official or trusted domains. The links use HTTP instead of HTTPS, which indicates lack of security. The domain names are unusual and likely created for phishing purposes.

b) Content Analysis  
The emails use emotional and psychological techniques:
- Email 1 creates urgency (“Account will be locked”)
- Email 2 creates excitement (“You won a prize”)
- Email 3 creates fear (“Suspicious login attempt”)

These tactics are commonly used in phishing attacks to pressure users into acting quickly without thinking.

c) Technical Analysis  
Using tools such as whois, nslookup, and curl in Kali Linux, the domains appear suspicious and not associated with legitimate organizations. This indicates they are likely used for malicious purposes.

4. PHISHING INDICATORS

The following indicators were identified:

- Use of urgent or threatening language  
- Suspicious and non-official domain names  
- Lack of HTTPS security  
- Generic greetings such as “Dear User”  
- Requests to click links immediately  
- Emotional manipulation (fear, urgency, reward)

5. RISK CLASSIFICATION

All three emails are classified as:

PHISHING

6. HOW THE ATTACK WORKS

The attacker sends a fake email designed to look legitimate. The email includes a malicious link that redirects the user to a fake website. When the user enters their personal information, the attacker collects and uses it for fraud or unauthorized access.

7. PREVENTION METHODS

To protect against phishing attacks, users should:

- Avoid clicking on unknown or suspicious links  
- Always verify the sender’s email address  
- Check if the website uses HTTPS  
- Be cautious of urgent or threatening messages  
- Use security tools such as antivirus and spam filters  

8. DO’S AND DON’TS

DO:
- Verify email sources before taking action  
- Report suspicious emails to IT or security teams  
- Use strong and unique passwords  

DON’T:
- Click on unknown links  
- Share personal or sensitive information  
- Trust emails that create panic or excitement  

9. CONCLUSION

Phishing attacks remain one of the most common cybersecurity threats. By analyzing email content, links, and behavior patterns, it is possible to identify and prevent such attacks. Awareness and caution are the best defenses against phishing.
